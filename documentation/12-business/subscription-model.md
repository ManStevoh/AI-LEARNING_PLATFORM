# Subscription Model

## Revenue Strategy

The platform should support both individual and institution-based revenue. The most strategic early path is institutional partnerships because schools, academies, NGOs, and training programs can bring groups of learners at once and create credibility.

## Customer Segments

- individual learners,
- parents and families,
- teachers,
- schools,
- coding academies,
- TVET institutions,
- universities,
- NGOs,
- governments,
- corporate training programs.

## Plan Types

### Free Learner

Purpose:

- lead generation,
- trial learning,
- community reach,
- brand growth.

Possible limits:

- limited AI questions per day,
- limited projects,
- limited courses,
- no advanced code review,
- no certificate or limited certificates,
- community support only.

### Student Pro

Purpose:

- individual learner monetization.

Features:

- higher AI limits,
- coding mentor,
- project generator,
- interview practice,
- portfolio guidance,
- certificates,
- advanced courses.

### Teacher Pro

Purpose:

- monetize individual teachers and small tutors.

Features:

- lesson planner,
- quiz generator,
- rubric generator,
- assignment feedback,
- classroom resources,
- limited student groups.

### Institution Starter

Purpose:

- small schools, coding clubs, community centers.

Features:

- discounted learner seats,
- teacher dashboard,
- class management,
- assignments,
- basic analytics,
- institution billing.

### Institution Growth

Purpose:

- larger schools, bootcamps, and TVET institutions.

Features:

- more seats,
- multiple teachers,
- AI usage pool,
- advanced analytics,
- certificates,
- reports,
- bulk import,
- priority support.

### Institution Enterprise

Purpose:

- universities, NGOs, governments, corporate programs.

Features:

- custom pricing,
- dedicated support,
- custom curriculum mapping,
- advanced reports,
- integrations,
- data processing agreements,
- possible dedicated deployment,
- regional compliance requirements.

### Family Plan

Purpose:

- parents with multiple children.

Features:

- multiple learner profiles,
- parent dashboard,
- progress reports,
- shared billing,
- age-appropriate learning paths.

## Entitlements

Every plan should define:

- learner seats,
- teacher seats,
- AI usage quota,
- course access,
- project limits,
- code execution limits,
- storage limits,
- analytics level,
- certificate access,
- support level,
- offline access,
- export access,
- integration access.

## Payment Methods

Kenya-first:

- M-Pesa,
- cards where available,
- bank transfer for institutions,
- invoices for institutions.

Global:

- Stripe,
- cards,
- invoices,
- bank transfer,
- possible local payment partners by country.

## Institutional Discounting

Discount logic should support:

- volume discounts,
- annual discounts,
- NGO discounts,
- government contracts,
- pilot pricing,
- scholarship seats,
- country-specific pricing,
- low-income school pricing.

## Billing Requirements

Required billing capabilities:

- plans,
- subscriptions,
- trials,
- coupons,
- invoices,
- payment retries,
- grace periods,
- seat assignment,
- usage metering,
- AI quota tracking,
- payment webhooks,
- tax fields,
- reconciliation,
- refunds,
- cancellations,
- upgrade/downgrade workflows.

## Example Pricing Model

Final prices require market validation. Early example bands:

- Free: limited access.
- Student Pro: affordable monthly plan for individuals.
- Teacher Pro: monthly plan for individual teachers.
- Institution Starter: priced per learner seat or learner band.
- Institution Growth: discounted annual school contract.
- Enterprise: custom.

## Key Business Metrics

- monthly recurring revenue,
- annual recurring revenue,
- active learners,
- active institutions,
- learner retention,
- course completion,
- AI usage per learner,
- gross margin after AI cost,
- customer acquisition cost,
- lifetime value,
- churn,
- expansion revenue,
- seat utilization,
- teacher activation,
- institution renewal rate.

## Strategic Recommendation

Build subscription infrastructure early enough to support institutional pilots, but do not overcomplicate enterprise billing before the first schools validate willingness to pay. The first commercial version should support:

- institution accounts,
- seat-based subscriptions,
- teacher and learner roles,
- M-Pesa payments,
- manual invoice option,
- AI usage limits,
- and admin-controlled entitlements.
