# Go-To-Market And Pilot Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines how the platform should validate demand, run school/institution pilots, prove learning value, and convert early partners into paying customers.

## Initial Target Market

Start with Kenya.

Best early segments:

- private primary schools,
- coding academies,
- STEM clubs,
- homeschool groups,
- NGO digital skills programs,
- TVET/college digital skills departments.

## Early Value Proposition

For schools:

- teach coding without needing many expert software instructors,
- reduce teacher preparation time,
- track learner progress by skill,
- provide modern AI-supported digital skills,
- create projects learners can show parents.

For teachers:

- ready lessons,
- block coding projects,
- AI lesson support,
- easier grading,
- visibility into struggling learners.

For learners:

- fun block coding,
- AI mentor,
- games and animations,
- progress badges,
- path toward real software engineering.

For parents:

- visible progress,
- future career relevance,
- safe guided technology learning.

## Pilot Offer

Recommended pilot:

- 6-8 weeks,
- 1-3 institutions,
- 50-300 learners,
- 3-10 teachers,
- Level 1 block coding,
- teacher dashboard,
- basic AI mentor,
- final showcase project.

## Pilot Success Metrics

Measure:

- learner activation rate,
- weekly active learners,
- lesson completion,
- final project completion,
- teacher login frequency,
- AI mentor helpfulness rating,
- common support issues,
- teacher time saved,
- parent/institution satisfaction,
- willingness to pay.

## Pilot Deliverables

Provide:

- onboarding session,
- teacher guide,
- learner accounts,
- class setup,
- weekly support,
- final project showcase,
- pilot report,
- pricing proposal.

## Conversion Strategy

At end of pilot:

- present learner projects,
- present skill mastery report,
- present teacher time-saved report,
- present institution dashboard,
- offer annual discounted school subscription,
- offer teacher training package.

## Pricing Validation

Test:

- per learner per term,
- per learner per year,
- school seat bundles,
- NGO cohort pricing,
- family plan for individual learners.

Avoid:

- pricing too low to cover AI/support costs,
- pricing too high for early adoption,
- unlimited AI without usage controls.

## Sales Materials Needed

Required:

- landing page,
- school brochure,
- teacher demo video,
- learner project examples,
- pricing sheet,
- pilot proposal,
- data privacy summary,
- AI safety summary,
- curriculum alignment summary.

## Partnership Channels

Potential partners:

- private schools,
- coding academies,
- NGOs,
- county education offices,
- teacher training programs,
- universities,
- TVET institutions,
- telcos,
- device suppliers,
- cloud providers,
- youth organizations.

## Customer Success

Early customers need:

- onboarding,
- teacher training,
- WhatsApp/email support,
- usage check-ins,
- monthly report,
- feedback loop,
- renewal conversation.

## Risks

Risks:

- teacher resistance,
- unreliable internet/devices,
- weak onboarding,
- AI cost overruns,
- learners using AI to shortcut,
- school budget constraints,
- unclear curriculum fit.

Mitigations:

- teacher training,
- offline worksheets,
- AI usage limits,
- clear curriculum mapping,
- low-bandwidth design,
- pilot pricing,
- strong final showcase.

## First 12-Month Commercial Goal

Target:

- 10 pilot institutions,
- convert 3-5 to paid,
- 1,000+ learners reached,
- publish learner project showcase,
- validate pricing,
- refine Level 1 and Level 2 curriculum,
- build repeatable onboarding playbook.

## Definition Of Done

Pilot strategy is ready when:

- pilot package exists,
- onboarding materials exist,
- success metrics exist,
- privacy/safety docs exist,
- pricing proposal exists,
- support process exists,
- final report template exists.
